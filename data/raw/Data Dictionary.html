Getting Started

Key Notes & Tips

Data Quality Notes

  • age, declared_annual_income, occupation_category are null for corporate customers. Use declared_annual_turnover and industry_code instead.
  • counterparty_bank_country is null for ~97% of transactions (only populated for international wires).
  • merchant_category_code is only present for card_payment transactions.
  • The status column indicates whether a transaction was approved (funds moved) or declined (attempted but failed). ~98% of transactions are approved.
  • Some customers have stale income declarations that may not reflect current reality.

Class Imbalance

  • ~5% of customers have confirmed suspicious activity (per compliance audit)
  • Your training set has ~25 suspicious out of 500
  • Class weighting, oversampling, or threshold tuning can help

Quick Start

The baselines.csv file provides pre-computed features. Merge it with customers.csv to train a baseline model immediately. Then improve by engineering your own features from raw transactions.

Internal Monitoring Threshold

NordikBank's TMS flags cash transactions above 15,000 DKK. This is the bank's own internal threshold, not the regulatory reporting limit. Structuring patterns cluster just below this amount.

The Core Challenge

The feature engineering is where the real work happens. The transactions table is deliberately granular. You must aggregate it into meaningful customer-level signals. The quality of your features determines the quality of your model.
