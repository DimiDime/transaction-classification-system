The Question
Five complications converge on one central challenge
Capacity
15,000 alerts/month. 45 analysts. Capacity for ~50 deep investigations. The math doesn't work.
Signal quality
97% false positive rate. Static rules can't tell a cash business from a structuring scheme.
Regulatory pressure
FSA review Q3 2025. Post-Danske Bank, explainable risk-based monitoring is mandatory.
Blind spots
Rules evaluate transactions in isolation — no behavioural history, no patterns over time.
Cost of misses
Under 6AMLD, the bank is liable for missed SAR filings — not the individual analyst.
⟹
Core question
How might we turn 15,000 noise alerts into 50 high-confidence investigations?
Reducing analyst burden while catching what matters — and explaining every decision to the FSA.
Our Answer
Our edge: a system that works for both the model and the analyst
⬡
Better model
Rank risk, don't just flag it
  • 64 behavioural features built from raw transactions — velocity, structuring, counterparty networks, geographic spread
  • XGBoost customer-level scoring with AUC-ROC 0.89 vs. 0.73 rule-based baseline
  • Prevalence-calibrated threshold: flags only the top ~4% of customers, matching analyst capacity
  • Per-customer feature contributions — every score has a receipt, not just a number

Better analysts

  • Prioritised alert queue: analysts see the highest-risk customers first — no more random triage
  • Customer investigation view: full behavioural profile, top drivers, and transaction history in one screen
  • SHAP-equivalent explanations satisfy FSA auditability — every flag is defensible in writing
  • Capacity-aligned threshold calibration means the queue is always within the team's reach
Our unique edge is not the model alone — it is the balance between technical performance and analyst wellbeing and stakeholder communication. Explainability is not a feature; it is the bridge between ML and compliance.
Modelling
XGBoost with engineered features delivers an AUC-ROC of 0.89 — 22% above the rule-based baseline
Four models evaluated
XGBoost Selected
  • Best val AUC-ROC and best PR-AUC — wins on both metrics
  • Smallest train–val gap (+0.11) — most generalisable
  • Native per-customer explanations satisfy FSA auditability
Random Forest — dropped
  • Similar AUC to LR/GB, no meaningful gain over XGBoost
  • Weaker PR-AUC — ranks the 9 val positives less precisely
Logistic Regression — dropped
  • Lowest AUC-ROC — linear model misses feature interactions
  • Retained as interpretability benchmark only
Gradient Boosting — dropped
  • Lowest PR-AUC (0.37) — poor precision on suspicious customers
  • No native imbalance parameter; sample weighting less effective
